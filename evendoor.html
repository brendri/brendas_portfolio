Summary

About Evendoor

Evendoor is an app designed to connect event organizers with small business owners, offering a centralized hub that simplifies the process of discovering opportunities. It acts as a seamless connection point for networks that are typically decentralized and require significant effort to find.

Problem

There is an information gap between small business owners and event organizers, making it challenging to connect. The complex, time-consuming search process can be overwhelming and hinder collaboration.

Our Solution

To address this, we created Evendoor—an app that empowers small business owners to expand as vendors while helping event organizers easily host successful, well-organized events.

Investigating the Problem

Started with an Interview Analysis

I conducted interviews with 2-3 diverse small business owners via Zoom, which helped me gain a deeper understanding of their unique challenges, operational methods, and the tools they use to manage and grow their businesses.


In my analysis, I found some common key themes and insights, such as:

  • Community Engagement: Businesses rely on both digital and in-person connections, emphasizing the need for tools that support flexible engagement.
  • Traditional vs. Digital Tools: Diverse operational methods highlight the importance of accommodating both traditional and digital workflows for accessibility.
  • Workflow Strategies: Effective tools like scheduling, inventory management, and order tracking are essential to support varied business operations.
  • Technology Design: A user-friendly platform should integrate features like digitizing workflows, communication tools, and flexible scheduling to enhance efficiency and community engagement.

Creating Personas

We selected 4 individuals from our interviews, including both event organizers and small business owners. Through collaborative exploration of themes and insights, we synthesized key takeaways into representative personas, Ellie Hansen and Mei Ling Chen, highlighting their distinct challenges and unique goals.

Exploring Scenarios

  1. Mei Ling Chen struggles to manage event schedules and customer interactions for her small bakery, UM Sweet, while balancing her family responsibilities.
    Event Hub helps Mei Ling Chen streamline her event management by centralizing scheduling, providing easy access to event registrations, and offering automatic reminders for deadlines, allowing her to focus more on her bakery and less on logistics.
  2. Ellie struggles with establishing a firm line of communication and finding popular vendors to participate in the fair.
    Evendoor helps Ellie streamline her vendor outreach by connecting her directly with small businesses and providing a platform for clear, efficient communication.
  3. Marcus struggles to promote his sports vintage shop and connect with the Detroit community until he discovers Event Hub, which helps him find and register for local events.
    Event Hub helps Marcus easily discover and register for local sports events, allowing him to connect with the Detroit community and promote his vintage sports shop.

Shaping the Design

User Flows

After collecting all the data, paper design flows were used for quick iteration, which were later transformed into more detailed digital versions with multiple screens and interactions. These user flows helped outline the user's journey through the product, ensuring smooth transitions between lo-fi and hi-fi prototyping.

Sketches

Sketches, similar to paper diagrams, were created quickly to generate a variety of ideas in a short period. None of these drawings were intended to be perfect, as many readings emphasize that they don't need to be exceptional.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Ideas and flows were transitioned into Figma as a low-fidelity version. Throughout the development process, feedback from peers and usability testing revealed several navigation issues.

Final Design

Onboarding

Small businesses can start using Evendoor by completing a Vendor Application, which distinguishes them as independent entities while events are treated as time-bound. The application remains editable throughout their time on the platform, allowing vendors to update their information as needed.

Design Flow - For Small Businesses

SBOs use a tailored interface with advanced filters for targeted event searches, viewing key details and options to apply, favorite, or explore similar events.

Design Flow - For Small Businesses

The Calendar feature organizes events as "Saved" or "Registered," providing detailed information and simplifying event management for SBOs.

Design Flow - For Event Organizers

Event Organizers (EOs) use a tailored interface with filters to refine searches by categories like food, offering a carousel view for quick browsing and flexible search options.

Design Flow - For Event Organizers

The Calendar helps Event Organizers (EOs) track and manage events by categorizing them as "Posted" or "Achieved." EOs can view event details and use the Recruited Vendors List to track vendor invitations and responses, including accepted, declined, and pending.

Learnings

Iteration = Value

I learned the importance of iteration by understanding how consistently testing ideas, gathering feedback, and refining designs help better meet user needs. It taught me the value of adaptability and the need to embrace feedback as a crucial part of the design process to create more intuitive and user-centered solutions.


Teamwork Makes the Design Work

I learned the importance of collaboration skills. It is essential to consistently update on changes and remain open to new ideas. By fostering stronger collaboration through team discussions, feedback sessions, and recognizing how diverse perspectives enhance the design process.
