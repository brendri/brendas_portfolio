I'm Brenda. It's so nice to meet you!

A recent grad who turned her curiosity into direction, now mastering UX Design and Research to take the next step toward designing experiences that empower all.

More about me

My friends and I outside of Union

I recently graduated from University of Michigan with my Bachelors in Science of Information (UX Design) with a minor in Education for Empowerment and Urban Studies.


My passion is deeply rooted in empathy and a commitment to advocating for fairness. I strive to represent underserved communities and give them a voice through my work. By designing inclusive and meaningful solutions, I aim to address social inequities and create experiences that empower those who are often overlooked.


My focus is on understanding the unique needs of users and fostering voice and inclusivity to promote positive social change.

My Design Philosophy

As I connect with people and listen to their experiences, I focus on what is needed. I've learned that empathy, community, and equity shape the conversations and experiences we share. These principles guide my work, helping me translate someone's vision into life.

Empathy

Designing with a deep understanding of users' emotions and needs to create meaningful solutions.

Community

Fostering connection, collaboration, and support through designs that bring people together.

Equity

Amplifying voices to ensure fairness and opportunity in every design.

The Other Side of Me

If I'm not designing, I'm painting away

Painting has always been a favorite pastime of mine, a way to unwind and let my creativity flow. Inspired by Pinterest or market finds. Instead of buying, I love the challenge of making it myself. One of my prized possessions is a three-dimensional piece I created as a class project, showcasing different views of Detroit. It reminds me of the beauty in the city and the power of art to tell a story. Most of my paintings become gifts for family and friends because gifting art is how I show my love.
