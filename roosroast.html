RoosRoast Coffee: Mirroring the vibrant community-focused experience to online shopping

Summary

About RoosRoast Coffee

RoosRoast is an eclectic coffee shop and roasting company that prides itself in its dedication to serving and enriching the local Ann Arbor community.

Problem

The RoosRoast website struggles to reflect the vibrant, community-focused personality of the shop, with underemphasized subscription services, inconsistent branding, and a lack of engaging features. Additionally, unclear distinctions between order types, disconnected transaction systems, and missing product highlights like tea and low-sugar options hinder usability and customer engagement.

Our Solution

To address this, we will implement product categorization/hierarchy, personalize the shopping experience, and emphasize subscription features to encourage user interaction.

Investigating the Problem

Started with an Interview Analysis

I conducted a competitive review between three different coffee shops, analyzing their subscription features to identify key takeaways and design considerations for enhancing RoosRoast's subscription experience.


These included clearly distinguishing subscriptions from single-sales, providing easy options for type, frequency, and grind that are editable, displaying subscription contents with previews and pricing, placing a subscription tab on the menu for visibility, allowing personalized pricing, and adding a section to explain the subscription breakdown and purchasing steps.

Baymard Institute

Throughout my research, I leaned on the Baymard Institute as a critical resource for data-driven insights and usability guidelines to optimize RoosRoast's eCommerce experience. I applied their recommendations to key areas of the website to enhance usability and efficiency:

  • Homepage: Implemented clear, full-scope links for better navigation and visibility (Ref. #958).
  • Shopping Cart: Designed the cart to support users who use it as both a comparison and shopping tool (Ref. #620).
  • Checkout: Provided a clear step-by-step visual guide for the checkout process (Ref. #611).
  • Product Page: Styled the "Add to Cart" button prominently, ensuring it stood out as the primary action (Ref. #791).

Shaping the Design

Card Sorting

After collecting all the data, a card sorting diagram of the navigation was created and used for quick iteration, which was later transformed into more detailed digital versions with interactions. The card sorting diagram helped outline the most intuitive grouping and hierarchy for the navigation, ensuring the final design aligned with user expectations and facilitated easy access to key content.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Ideas and flows were transitioned into Figma as a low-fidelity version. Throughout the development process, feedback from peers highlighted details about color usage, shaping the design to align with accessibility standards and aesthetic preferences while refining the overall user experience

Design System

Upon completing and iterating on the low-fidelity wireframes, the design system for RoosRoast Coffee began taking shape by coordinating color contrasts with the existing RoosRoast website palette. The process focused on interactive elements designed to capture user attention, emphasizing key details like icons and controls.

Key Design Features

Checkout Segmentation

Effortlessly guides checkout while showcasing RoosRoast's character through options and flow.

Quirks/ Personality

Showcases personality and charm effortlessly through its origin, flavor, and brewing tips.

Shopping Cart

With intuitive features, the shopping cart makes managing selected items and completing transactions straightforward and hassle-free.
